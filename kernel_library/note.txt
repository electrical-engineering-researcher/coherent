Note: The kernel library consists of 157 reusable RTL kernels grouped into three categories. 
(1) Human-written reusable primitives (62 kernels), 
developed and reviewed by experienced RTL engineers, 
including parameterized counters, shift registers, FIFOs, 
synchronizers, arbiters, UART blocks, clock dividers, 
FSM templates, and datapath operators. Some of these kernels originated from 
industrial RTL design experience and were later generalized into reusable forms; 
due to confidentiality agreements, the originating organizations and 
engineers cannot be disclosed. 
(2) Benchmark-derived reusable modules (71 kernels), 
extracted and generalized from publicly available sources 
including RTL-LLM benchmark designs, OpenCores components, 
and educational RTL examples after removing task-specific logic 
and normalizing interfaces. 
(3) Curated design patterns (24 kernels), consisting of reusable architectural 
templates such as CDC synchronizers, handshake controllers, 
pipelined arithmetic units, resource-sharing structures, protocol adapters, 
and FSM skeletons. All kernels were validated through compilation, simulation,
and synthesis checks before inclusion in the library.
